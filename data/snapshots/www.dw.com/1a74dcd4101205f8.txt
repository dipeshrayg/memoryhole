Prominent Cuban dissident artist Luis Manuel Otero Alcantara, whose five-year prison sentence ended last week, has arrived in the United States, according to US Secretary of State Marco Rubio.
Rubio did not provide the whereabouts of the 38-year-old artist, or say exactly when he arrived.
A video posted on Otero Alcantara's official Facebook page, which is managed by his close friends, also showed his arrival in the United States.
"After five years of unjust imprisonment, Cuban artist Luis Manuel Otero Alcantara has finally been freed," read a post.
The post also said he was held in a maximum-security prison and that a condition of his release was "permanent departure from the island."
On Friday, Otero Alcantara had been granted a humanitarian visa to travel to the US after serving his prison sentence.
Who is Luis Manuel Otero Alcantara?
Otero Alcantara is one of the most visible faces of the San Isidro Movement, a group of artists and intellectuals seeking greater freedom of expression in the communist country.
The Cuban artist was arrested in July 2021, as he was leaving his home to join a massive protest against blackouts in the capital, Havana.
In 2022, a court sentenced him to five years in prison for insulting national symbols, contempt and disturbing the public order.
"Otero Alcantara's only 'crime' was refusing to stay silent and using his art to demand the basic freedoms everyday Cubans have been denied for almost seven decades," Rubio said in a statement.
The Cuban government has accused Otero Alcantara of acting on behalf of Washington to destabilize the communist-run island.
