US attacked IRGC base in north: state media
Iran said that the US military hit a Revolutionary Guards' (IRGC) base in the northern province of Gilan, according to the state media.
"This morning, an IRGC naval forces headquarters in Zibakenar was targeted by American enemy projectiles, which damaged some of the equipment in the complex," said Ali Bagheri, deputy governor for the province, quoted by the official IRNA news agency.
He added, "Based on initial investigations, no reports of human casualties have been received so far, and the various aspects of this incident are being investigated by the relevant authorities."
Iran's FM Abbas Araghchi warns countries assisting US attacks
Speaking at the Shanghai Cooperation Organization summit in Kyrgyzstan, Iran's Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi said Tehran would hold accountable any country assisting the US in an attack on the Islamic republic.
"Any participation, cooperation or assistance in committing aggression against Iran will create international responsibility for the relevant parties," Araghchi said, adding that Iran "reserves its right to take all necessary measures within the framework of self-defense."
He said the source of any aggression against Iran would be considered as a legitimate target of Iran's defense forces.
US strikes on southwest kill at least four people, says Iran
The overnight US attack on the province of Khuzestan in southwestern Iran killed at least four people and injured several others, Iran's official IRNA news agency reported.
Khuzestan deputy Governor Valiollah Hayati said that the US missile attack hit areas around provincial capital Ahvaz.
"Four of our compatriots were martyred and five others were injured," IRNA cited Hayati as saying.
Bahrain sounds sirens
Bahrain activated sirens early Friday, alerting residents to take shelter from detected attacks.
The sirens were activated hours after the US said it had launched a new series of strikes on Iran, continuing its attack for the 13th consecutive night.
The Gulf states have come under frequent fire over the past week as the US and Iran exchange escalating strikes.
Germany's Merz condemns Houthi's Red Sea attack
German Chancellor Friedrich Merz has strongly condemned attacks by Yemen's Houthi militia on Saudi ships in the Red Sea.
He called for an immediate end to the attacks and described any form of naval blockade as "unacceptable."
"These reckless attacks endanger freedom of navigation and threaten to further undermine regional stability," Merz said in a statement on Thursday.
Merz expressed Berlin's solidarity with Saudi Arabia and its other partners in the region.
Trump says Iran will pay for damage to shipping
US President Donald Trump said Iran would be held responsible for the financial cost of any damage to ships or cargo amid the dispute over the Strait of Hormuz.
He said he would use seized Iranian assets to pay for any damage to shipping.
"From this point forth, any and all damages done to Ships, Cargo, or anything related thereto, will be paid for by Iranian Money that the United States has in its possession, and controls," Trump wrote on his Truth Social platform.
"These damages may be very substantial but, nevertheless, this is the fair and equitable thing to do," Trump added.
Trump's statement comes after Iran-backed Houthis said they attacked two Saudi Arabian oil tankers in the Red Sea.
In response, Iran warned that Trump's threat to use seized Iranian assets would set a dangerous precedent.
"Seizing another nation's assets to pay for unrelated future claims is an incendiary precedent," Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi posted on X.
US strikes Iran for 13th night in a row
The US has said it launched strikes on Iran for the 13th consecutive night, as Iranian state media reported explosions across the country.
The US Central Command (CENTCOM) said that it targeted Iran's military command centers, drone storage facilities, communication networks, coastal surveillance sites, and maritime capabilities.
CENTCOM said the strikes were launched "to hold Iran accountable and diminish threats from the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps to commercial shipping" in the Strait of Hormuz.
Tensions between Iran and the US have escalated over shipping routes since a ceasefire collapsed earlier this month.
CENTCOM also said that it had redirected 12 commercial vessels and disabled one to prevent ships from entering or leaving Iranian ports or coastal areas since "resuming the naval blockade against Iran nine days ago."
The latest US attacks come after Yemen's Iran-backed Houthi rebels said they targeted two Saudi oil tankers in the Red Sea.
President Donald Trump on Thursday threatened "major military punishment" against the Houthis if their attacks on ships continue.
