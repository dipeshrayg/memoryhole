Diplomatic exchanges with US ongoing despite strikes - Iran
Iran said on Monday that despite the recent hostilities, diplomatic exchanges were still taking place between Tehran and Washington via mediators.
"We have been informed by mediators, we have received messages - without going into details - but the main point is that the diplomatic apparatus has been active in recent days and ideas have been conveyed to us by certain mediators," Iranian Foreign Ministry spokesman Esmaeil Baghaei said at a press conference.
At the same press conference, Baghaei responded to a question as to whether the door to diplomacy with the US was now closed by saying that Tehran considered both diplomacy and war as ways of furthering national interests.
"I reject the duality of either war or diplomacy," he said.
"Diplomacy is a tool through which we pursue our national interests, just like war," he added.
Vessel adrift off Oman after projectile hit - UK maritime agency
An unknown projectile has hit a vessel 8 nautical miles (14.8 km) northwest of the village of Kumzar in Oman, leaving it adrift after the crew safely abandoned ship, the United Kingdom Maritime Trade Operations (UKMTO) agency said on Monday.
Earlier, the agency had said the vessel was on fire.
The fire has not been put out, but no environmental impact was reported, the UKMTO said.
The vessel was identified by the British maritime risk management group Vanguard as the Malta-flagged tanker Kavomaleas, operated by operated by Greek shipping firm Dynacom.
Iran's Revolutionary Guards said on Monday that two oil tankers trying to transit the Strait of Hormuz "exploded and were brought to a halt," but it is not clear whether this is connected with the incident reported by the UKTMO.
In recent weeks, Iran has hit tankers sailing a route through the strait that sticks close to the Omani coast in a bid to force vessels to use its own waters and pay for safe passage.
US strikes kill one in northwest, hit nuclear-plant city of Bushehr - state media
Overnight US strikes in northwestern Iran have killed at least one person and wounded several others, Iranian state media reported Monday.
"One person was martyred and several others were injured in a terrorist and aggressive American attack on an area southwest of Tabriz on Monday morning," Majid Farshi, the head of crisis management of East Azerbaijan province, told the state news agency IRNA.
Iranian state media also said that several locations in the southern port city of Bushehr were hit by US strikes.
The city is home to Iran's only civilian nuclear plant.
Bahrain's air defenses intercepting attack from Iran - state TV
Bahrain has come under aerial attack from Iran after missile alert sirens sounded in the island kingdom, with air defenses working to repel the assault, state TV has said.
Journalists from the AFP news agency reported that explosions were heard in the capital, Manama, after the sirens went off.
Bahrain is home to the US Navy's 5th fleet, making it a frequent target for Tehran as it retaliates for recent US strikes on the Islamic Republic.
Ryanair reports war-induced profit drop
Irish low-budget airline Ryanair said on Monday that its net profit fell 34% in its fiscal first quarter under the impact of the Middle East conflict, which has raised jet fuel prices and caused the carrier to reduce the price of fares.
In a statement, the airline said profit after tax dropped to €538 million ($616 million) in the three months to the end of June, down from €820 million a year earlier.
"Operating costs rose 11% to €3.81 billion as the price of our 20% unhedged jet fuel more than doubled" in the first quarter, chief executive Michael O'Leary said in the earnings statement.
Although passenger numbers were up by 6%, this was offset by a 6% reduction in fares.
O'Leary said fares "required stimulation as the Middle East conflict led to consumer hesitancy, concerns about EU jet-fuel shortages, economic uncertainty and later bookings."
He added that Ryanair's net profit for the rest of its financial year "remains highly sensitive to ... conflict escalation in the Middle East and Ukraine, the price of unhedged jet-fuel, macro-economic shocks" and European air traffic control strikes.
Iran strikes Kuwait, missile alert sirens sound in Bahrain
Iran has announced strikes on Kuwait and sirens have gone off in Bahrain to warn of a retaliatory strike after the latest round of US airstrikes on the Islamic Republic.
Iran's Revolutionary Guard on Monday said they hit US military targets in Kuwait and "completely destroyed" an early-warning radar system. They also said they struck an MQ-9 drone hangar at Ali Al Salem Air Base, setting several drones on fire.
In Bahrain, the US Embassy early on Monday warned that "Iran may seek to target unspecified locations in central Manama," the capital, but gave no further details.
Bahrain, a small island kingdom in the Persian Gulf, is home to the US Navy's 5th fleet and has come under attack by Iran several times since the interim deal between Washington and Iran on ending the war has broken down.
US concludes strikes, Trump says Iran again hit 'very hard'
The US military hit Iranian military coastal surveillance and air defense facilities in the ninth round of strikes, US Central Command (CENTCOM) said. The strikes have now been concluded.
The strikes targeted Iranian military command centers, air defense and coastal surveillance sites, maritime capabilities, missile and drone launch sites, and communications networks, CENTCOM said in a post on X.
US President Donald Trump said that the latest strikes against Iran were carried out in response to the deaths of US service members in the region.
"Those great people, those great patriots, were out there fighting that Iran cannot have a nuclear weapon," Trump told reporters after returning from the FIFA World Cup final.
"We hit them very hard again tonight."
Trump said the strikes had been carried out "in honor" of the fallen troops. He also said Iran had suffered heavy military losses in the conflict.
"They've lost everything almost militarily," Trump said.
Trump also claimed the United States controlled the Strait of Hormuz.
"We control the strait. They don't control anything. So we'll see what happens," he said.
However, Iran has refuted that claim, saying a framework agreement reached in mid-June gives Tehran sole responsibility for administering the strategic waterway. That agreement has since fallen apart amid the new hostilities.
Vessel on fire in Strait of Hormuz, UK maritime agency says
The United Kingdom Maritime Trade Operations agency said that a vessel is on fire 8 nautical miles (14.8 kilometers) northwest of Oman's Kumzar in the Strait of Hormuz.
"UKMTO has received a report of an incident 8NM northwest of Kumzar, Oman. UKMTO have received information from military authorities that a vessel is on fire," the British organization said in a statement.
It added that the cause of the fire had not been verified.
US military member killed in Iraq during 'controlled demolition' of Iranian drone
A US service member was killed in Iraq during a controlled demolition of an Iranian drone, the US military said late Sunday night.
The service member is the third American to be killed in recent days, after two US troops were killed in Jordan on Friday in a strike claimed by Iran's Revolutionary Guards.
A third service member was missing after that strike. The US military said Sunday that "unidentified remains" had been found and were being examined.
At least 17 US military personnel have been killed since the war began in late February.
US strikes Iran for 9th consecutive night
The US military launched a new wave of strikes targeting Iran early on Monday, the ninth consecutive night of attacks.
"The strikes will continue degrading Iranian military capabilities used to attack commercial vessels and civilian mariners transiting the Strait of Hormuz," the US military's Central Command (CENTCOM) said in a post on X.
Iranian state media reported that the northwestern city of Tabriz was struck. Previous waves of US attacks in the latest escalation had mainly targeted sites along the Persian Gulf coast in southern Iran.
The US has resumed daily attacks on Iran since a ceasefire collapsed on July 8.
