Israeli civilian kills two West Bank Palestinians behind stabbing, army says
An Israeli civilian killed two Palestinians in the occupied West Bank after they allegedly stabbed another Israeli who was trying to extinguish a wildfire near a settlement, the military said.
Violence in the West Bank, a Palestinian territory occupied by Israel since 1967, has escalated since the start of the war in Gaza.
"Earlier today, a fire broke out in the area adjacent to the community of Itamar. During the extinguishing attempts, two terrorists stabbed an Israeli civilian," the military said.
It added that another Israeli identified the attack and "eliminated" the two Palestinians, without specifying how they were killed.
The wounded Israeli was stabbed in the chest and evacuated to hospital in serious condition, health officials said.

Trump says Saudi nuclear deal contingent on recognising Israel
President Donald Trump has said the nuclear deal between the US and Saudi Arabia signed Wednesday is contingent on the Gulf kingdom recognising Israel.
The deal to establish a civilian nuclear programme in the Gulf country "will be approved, but is totally subject to Saudi Arabia joining the very respected and successful Abraham Accords," Trump said on his Truth Social platform.
Kuwait says drone attack hits Abdali border crossing with Iraq
Kuwait's army has said the Abdali border crossing with Iraq was hit by a drone attack, causing material damage but no casualties.
Located in Kuwait's northern Al-Jahra Province, Abdali is the country's main land border crossing with Iraq and a key route for passenger and commercial traffic between the two countries.
EU urges Houthis to stop attacks in Red Sea
Iran's Houthi allies in Yemen must cease all actions that endanger international shipping and the lives of seafarers, EU foreign policy chief Kaja Kallas said on Thursday.
Recent threats by Yemen's Houthis to impose a maritime blockade on Saudi Arabia "pose a direct threat to regional stability and constitute a dangerous escalation", Kallas said in a statement.
The Houthis on Thursday said they struck two Saudi oil tankers as part of a blockade on Saudi Arabia, threatening to create a second chokepoint on global oil supplies alongside the Strait of Hormuz.
Kallas said such attacks were "unacceptable" and inflamed the already fragile situation in the region.
"Navigation must be unimpeded, in the Strait of Hormuz as well as in the Red Sea," the EU's top diplomat said.
UK withdraws remaining diplomats from Iran
Britain said on Friday it had withdrawn remaining diplomatic staff from Iran as the conflict between Iran and the United States escalates.
"Due to the security situation, UK staff have been temporarily withdrawn from Iran. Our embassy continues to operate remotely," the Foreign Office said on its website.
Britain in June withdrew its embassy staff from Iran, following in the footsteps of other European nations as well as Australia and New Zealand.
The withdrawals came after Israel launched renewed air strikes on Iran claiming its arch enemy was on the verge of developing a nuclear weapon.
Kremlin declines comment on report that Russia may have helped Iran strike CIA facilities in Gulf
The Kremlin on Thursday declined to comment on a Reuters report that US intelligence analysts were investigating possible Russian assistance in Iranian drone attacks on CIA facilities in the Gulf.
Four people familiar with US intelligence told Reuters that the officials had not yet reached firm conclusions about the possible Russian involvement in the attacks on CIA facilities.
But they cited the strikes' effectiveness and apparent precision, as well as Russia's broader technical support for Iran, as possible evidence.
Oman says it is working with Saudi Arabia, Yemeni parties and UN to resume political track to stability
Oman said on Thursday it is coordinating with Saudi Arabia, Yemeni parties and the UN special envoy for Yemen to resume the political track aimed at achieving security and stability in the region, according to a foreign ministry statement.
The ministry also stressed the need to avoid escalation and any threats that could worsen the situation in the Red Sea and endanger freedom of navigation.

Jordan army says it intercepted Iranian missiles, drones
Jordan said on Thursday that it had intercepted Iranian missiles and drones, as Tehran responded to US air strikes by hitting Washington's allies in the region.
The Jordanian army said in a statement it had intercepted three missiles on Thursday morning, while a fourth landed in an uninhabited area, and that it had intercepted six drones on Wednesday evening, with no casualties or material damage.

The Iranian army said Thursday that it would continue its strikes around the region until the attacks on Iran stopped, state television reported.

EasyJet profit slides on Iran war impact as suitors circle
Takeover target easyJet on Thursday said third-quarter profit slumped 70 percent, bruised by volatile fuel prices and a travel-wary consumer due to chaos caused by the Iran war, but indicated somewhat clearer skies heading into peak summer.
The budget airline reported an £85 million ($113.8 million) profit for the three months to June 30, a far cry from the £286 million it logged last year. Results of easyJet, and rival Ryanair earlier this week.
The British airline said, however, that consumer confidence was increasing into the peak summer period and there was strong demand from travellers booking tickets closer to the date of travel.
"As consumer confidence increases, we are seeing the load factor gap close for peak summer and an extension of the booking curve," CEO Kenton Jarvis said in a statement.
Oil prices rise for a fifth day on supply concerns amid chokepoint threats
Oil prices rose for a fifth day on Thursday on increasing concerns about supply availability amid attacks on tankers in the Red Sea by Yemen's Houthis and strikes between the US and Iran that have once again nearly shut the Strait of Hormuz.
Brent crude futures rose $2.42, or 2.6 percent, to $96.49 by 06:40 GMT, the highest since June 8, having settled up more than $3 at $94.07 in the previous session, just shy of a six-week high.
US West Texas Intermediate crude climbed $1.59, or 1.8 percent, to $88.42, after Wednesday's rise of 3 percent.
Iran Guards destroy US military equipment in Jordan
Iran's Revolutionary Guards, the ideological arm of the country's military, said on Thursday that they had attacked and destroyed several American military assets in Jordan.
The targets included a radar for the US THAAD missile defence system, a Patriot system, a C-RAM radar and a helicopter hangar, according to a statement from the Guards aired by state TV.
Iran's army says it attacked US bases in Kuwait, according to state media
Iran's army and its Revolutionary Guards on Thursday said they attacked several US bases in Kuwait following attacks by American forces on Iran.
The Guards claimed attacks on Ali Al Salem base, Camp Udairi and a telecommunications tower, while the army said it targeted Camp Doha, Ali Al Salem base and Camp Arifjan, according to separate statements by the forces carried by state TV.
Saudi Arabia confirms vessel hit in Red Sea by Houthi rebels
A vessel belonging to a Saudi company was hit while transiting the Red Sea, Saudi state media reported on Thursday, citing an official source, after Yemen's Houthi rebels claimed they had struck tankers in the waterway.
The source, quoted by the state-run Saudi Press Agency, said "the vessel ENCELIA, belonging to a Saudi company, was targeted while sailing in the Red Sea, resulting in a fire at the bow of the ship", but that all crew members were safe.
Iran says it stopped three tankers in Strait of Hormuz
Iran's Revolutionary Guards said Thursday they stopped three oil tankers from passing through the Strait of Hormuz, as the United States and Iran vie for control over the vital waterway.
In recent weeks, Iran has hit tankers using a route hugging the Omani coast to traverse the strait, in a bid to strong-arm vessels into using its own waters and paying for safe passage.
"Three oil tankers, provoked and tempted by the child-killing American army, were trying to pass through the mine-laid route south of the Strait of Hormuz, but after one of them exploded and caught fire, the other two quickly turned around and turned back," the Guards said in a statement quoted by Iranian state media.
The statement did not say when the incident happened.
"The powerful IRGC Navy emphasises that the Strait of Hormuz is under our control... and any ship that is deceived by the US and intends to pass without coordination with the Islamic Republic of Iran will suffer the same fate," the Guards added.
Houthis say they targeted two Saudi oil tankers
Yemen's Houthis said on Thursday they had carried out a military operation targeting two Saudi oil tankers, identified as ENCELA and LAYLIA, saying the vessels violated a naval blockade imposed by the group.
The Iran-aligned Houthis said on Monday they were imposing a naval blockade on Saudi Arabia, drawing a warning from the Saudi-led coalition that it would respond "firmly", in a move that threatens to disrupt global energy supplies beyond the Gulf.
