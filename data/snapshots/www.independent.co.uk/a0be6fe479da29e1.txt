Trump says he is close to decision on 'major military punishment' for Iran after Houthi tanker attacks
US president Donald Trump has said he is considering re-launching major combat operations against Iran and is close to a decision, telling Axios that Tehran "hasn't received enough pain yet."
Mr Trump's warning came after Yemen's Houthi fighters struck two Saudi oil tankers in the Red Sea, extending the Middle East war to a second major shipping chokepoint.
Mr Trump said he would hold Iran responsible for any further Houthi attacks, writing on social media: "If they do this again, the US will hold Iran responsible, in that the Houthis are a Surrogate and/or Proxy of Iran, and major military punishment will be inflicted upon Iran and, of course, the Houthis, themselves."
Sources in Iran and Yemen told Reuters that Iran had flown Revolutionary Guard commanders, military advisers and missile and drone-related equipment into Yemen days before the Houthis announced their Red Sea blockade. The Houthis dismissed the report.
