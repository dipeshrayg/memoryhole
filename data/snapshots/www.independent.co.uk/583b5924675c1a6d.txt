A fatal police shooting in Madison, Wisconsin, has fueled anger in the community as harrowing footage appeared to show the moment an officer killed a man at point-blank range in the middle of the street.
The victim, believed to be in his 30s and has not yet been identified, was seen in the cellphone footage struggling with several Madison Police Department officers before one of them fired the fatal shot Wednesday afternoon.
"This was an execution, and it never should have happened," said Fran Hong, a Democratic state representative running for governor.
Citizens reacted to the tragedy by marching on the State Capitol to protest, chanting, "Arrest the cops!" and later took over the stage at a downtown summer concert to draw attention to the killing, prompting the organizers to cancel the performance underway.
Madison Police Chief John Patterson warned the public against leaping to conclusions about the circumstances surrounding his death based on footage widely shared on social media.
Here is everything we know about the case so far
What happened?
Bystanders have shared disturbing footage of the shooting, that took place Wednesday at the busy intersection of South Baldwin and Williamson Streets around 1:34 p.m.
It showed the man, who police said was suspected of stealing bicycles and was armed with a knife, being pinned to the ground by several officers.
A struggle ensued and an officer yelled, "taser, taser taser," but was unable to discharge the weapon, police said.
Officers wrestled the man to the floor, when one of them pulled out his weapon and appeared to fire several shots at close range. The victim was then seen slumped on the ground and did not move again.
The Madison Police Department said in a statement that they had been responding to a report alleging that someone had been seen attempting to break into parked cars in the city's Marquette neighborhood.
Four officers then pursued the suspect, who fled on a bicycle, leading to a "physical struggle" at the intersection during which, police allege, the man resisted arrest and injured one of the officers with a "fix-blade knife," prompting them to Taser him, which proved "unsuccessful."
The injured officer, who the MPD said is "considered a veteran," then fatally shot the suspect, who was subsequently taken to hospital and pronounced dead.
A second officer was injured in the episode but police have not said how, only that they do not believe his wound was caused by the knife.
The four officers involved have since been placed on administrative leave according to procedure and the Wisconsin Department of Justice Division of Criminal Investigation has commenced an independent probe.
How has the community reacted?
Police Chief Patterson appealed for calm at a press conference in the aftermath, recounting the official narrative and pointing out that video of the shooting seen online only offered one side of the story.
He offered few details about the victim's identity, other than to say he was a man believed to be in his 30s.
