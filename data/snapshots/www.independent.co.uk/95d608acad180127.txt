Spain won the World Cup for the second time in their history as they claimed a 1-0 extra-time victory over 10-man Argentina before ugly scenes on the pitch after full time marred the celebrations.
Ferran Torres' smashed finish in the 106th minute was enough for Spain to add to their 2010 triumph but Argentina - who offered little throughout the match - reacted badly after the final whistle, with players brawling, Leandro Paredes sent off after grabbing Eric Garcia by the throat and reports of punches being thrown.
Donald Trump then unsurprisingly inserted himself into things post-match as he lurked towards the edge of Spain's trophy lift to ensure he was in the photo, despite Gianni Infantino trying to usher him away, after presenting the Spanish players with medals and the trophy. However, the US president was audibly booed when he first walked on to the pitch with Infantino.
After a dire spectacle for 90 minutes, with neither side mustering any real chances, Enzo Fernandez was sent off just before the end of normal time when he crunched Pau Cubarsi on halfway and was shown a second yellow card, having earlier been booked for dissent.
Spain then finally made the breakthrough when Torres calmly hammered home at the start of the second half of extra time. It meant Lionel Messi was denied on surely his final World Cup appearance, with the aftermath further souring the occasion for the 39-year-old football icon.

Argentina's World Cup final performance was a disgrace to Lionel Messi's legacy
Don't cry for them, Argentina. Some tears were shed by Lionel Messi, after an unsatisfactory end to one of the great World Cup careers. But his team-mates merit rather less sympathy. What had been a valiant, dramatic and compelling attempt to retain the World Cup ended with a lack of discipline, creativity and, above all, shots.
The historic feat, it transpired, was not becoming the first team since Brazil in 1962 to win consecutive World Cups, but in being officially the least attacking team ever in a final. Argentina had no shot of any kind in 90 minutes, an xG of 0.00 when it went to extra time. Spain had 20 shots before Argentina's first, in the 121st minute.
All this with a man who has been involved in more World Cup goals than anyone else, who many believe is the greatest footballer ever. A team with Messi should not be this dull, this focused on delaying and disrupting.

Ferran Torres's history moment echoes Andres Iniesta's 2010 World Cup-winning goal
Pedro Porro hung a cross into the sky and Argentina's defenders squinted at the ball like a dying sun, watching it set behind them. There, Nico Williams arrived by stealth to head it back into yawning space in the middle of the box, where Ferran Torres had momentarily slipped the attention of a limping Giuliano Simeone. Torres steadied himself before rapping his left foot through the ball, piercing a gap above Emiliano Martinez's gloves, and then he was off, adrenaline pumping, eyes bulging.
What an earth do you do when you've just won your country the World Cup? Marco Tardelli cried tears of joy. Andres Iniesta took his shirt off. Torres decided to murder a corner flag.
There was bedlam on the pitch and delirium in the stands. The cameras cut away from Torres's manic celebrations to show a group of grinning men in expensive suits. Sergio Ramos hugged David Villa, Iker Casillas rubbed Carles Puyol's long mane, and in amongst them was Iniesta himself, offering a little clap and smile.
