Andy Burnham must deliver emergency budget to 'reverse damage', says SNP
The SNP has called on Andy Burnham to deliver an emergency cost-of-living budget to "reverse damage" under the Labour government.
The former Greater Manchester mayor, who returned to Westminster last month as the MP for Makerfield, vowed to "bring back hope" in his speech as Labour leader on Friday.
SNP Westminster leader Dave Doogan said that Mr Burnham would be "judged on his actions not words" as the UK prepares for its seventh prime minister in 10 years.
Mr Doogan is calling on Mr Burnham to deliver an emergency cost-of-living budget when he enters No 10.
He continued: "While Westminster has been stuck in constant chaos, people's lives have got worse.
"People in Scotland don't want more empty promises about 'hope in their hearts' - they want money in their pockets and meaningful change to make people's lives and livelihoods better."
He urged Mr Burnham to deliver an emergency cost of living budget, and "a meaningful package of financial support for families, to reverse the damage done under the Labour Party government and put money back into people's pockets."
Andy Burnham: 'We're not in the right place as a country'
Andy Burnham has told The Times that "we're not in the right place as a country" ahead of becoming prime minister on Monday.
Referring to his pledge that he will set out a ten-year plan for the country, Mr Burnham said: "I'm not saying that means I'm setting out to say I'm going to be here for ten years... it's more that we're not in the right place structurally, we're not in the right place as a country. I think people can feel that."
He added: "To be clear to people, it can't be done overnight. But you've got to show people where we're going, and then the steps towards it. And that's what I'm going to try to do."
Speaking about his approach to the economy, Mr Burnham said: "I think you have to rethink how we run things to get a different approach to public spending, and to running the economy - more focused on early investment, early intervention, setting people up for success, and much less paying for failure."
Referring to his plans to scrap Sir Keir Starmer's digital ID scheme, Mr Burnham added: "That's an example of what's to come, freeing up resource for something that, in my view, wouldn't make a tangible difference to people's lives anytime soon. And even if it did, it wouldn't necessarily make life easier, it might do in the long run."

Burnham won't wear shirt and tie 'except in chamber or exceptional events' - report
Andy Burnham is set to forego wearing a shirt and tie as prime minister, according to reporting from the Sunday Times.
The Makerfield MP has opted for a black t shirt and jacket along his journey back into Westminister, and will reportedly have no stylistic change as he moves into 10 Downing Street on Monday.
The paper reported that he would only wear a shirt and tie in the chamber or for exceptional events.
