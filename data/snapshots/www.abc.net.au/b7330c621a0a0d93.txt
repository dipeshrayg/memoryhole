Indonesia sentences leader of baby-trafficking ring to seven years' jail
In short:
The leader of a baby-trafficking ring in Indonesia, who said she sent at least 12 babies to Singapore, has been sentenced to seven years in prison.
Lie Siu Luan, 70, the leader of a group of 19 people accused of trafficking babies, was found guilty of committing crimes "for the purpose of exploitation".
The sentence was lower than the 10 years demanded by prosecutors.
