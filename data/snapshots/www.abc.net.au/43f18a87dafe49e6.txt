Perth's road to nowhere deadlock resolved after four-year-delay over Aboriginal heritage concerns
In short:
A four-year dispute over Indigenous cultural heritage has been resolved, paving the way for a $180 million traffic bridge to complete an infamous "road to nowhere" in Midland.
The new 180-metre Lloyd Street Bridge avoids a culturally significant rock shelter and billabong near the Helena River after an agreement with traditional owners.
The Transport Minister says construction could begin by the end of the year.
