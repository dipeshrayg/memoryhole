Big Tech earnings season kicked off Wednesday with Google (GOOG, GOOGL) and Tesla (TSLA), and investors didn't like what they heard. Tesla tumbled 14% on Thursday to 11-month low as profit miss, capex spending outlook weigh on stock. Google fell more than 6% on capex growth expectations despite its earnings beat.
That wasn't the case for Intel, which reported its second quarter earnings after the bell on Thursday. Its results blew past Wall Street's expectations on both the top and bottom lines, and topped third quarter guidance estimates, sending the stock up more than 6% in after-hours trade.
Also on Thursday, AMD (AMD) officially launched its Helios rackscale system, with CEO Lisa Su announcing that the AI platform is in full production during the company's Advancing AI event in San Francisco, Calif.
The server cabinet combines 72 AMD Instinct MI455X graphics processing units (GPUs) with the company's Epyc central processing units (CPUs) into a rack designed to compete directly with Nvidia's (NVDA) Grace Blackwell and Vera Rubin-powered NVL72 rackscale systems. Nvidia's offering also has 72 GPUs.
AMD stock slipped more than 2% throughout Su's keynote.
